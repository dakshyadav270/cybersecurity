Common Cyber Threats

Understanding attacks is the first step to defending against them.

🎣

Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent emails or websites that trick users into revealing sensitive information like passwords and credit card numbers by impersonating trusted sources.

HIGH RISK
🦠

Malware & Ransomware

Malicious software that infiltrates systems to steal data, corrupt files, or encrypt your data and demand payment for its release.

HIGH RISK
🕵️

Social Engineering

Psychological manipulation that tricks people into breaking security procedures, often by impersonating IT staff or authority figures.

HIGH RISK
💥

DDoS Attacks

Distributed Denial of Service attacks flood servers with traffic from thousands of devices, making websites and services unavailable to real users.

MEDIUM RISK
🔓

Weak Passwords

Simple or reused passwords are cracked using brute-force tools in seconds. Poor password hygiene is one of the most preventable vulnerabilities.

HIGH RISK
📡

Man-in-the-Middle

Attackers intercept communications between two parties on unsecured networks (like public Wi-Fi) to eavesdrop or alter data in transit.

MEDIUM RISK
// Defense Strategies

How to Stay Protected

Simple habits that dramatically improve your security posture.

01

Use Strong Passwords

Create passwords with 12+ characters mixing uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse passwords across sites.

02

Enable 2FA Everywhere

Two-factor authentication adds a second layer — even if your password is stolen, attackers can't log in without your phone.

03

Keep Software Updated

Updates patch security vulnerabilities. Enable auto-updates for your OS, browser, and apps to stay protected automatically.

04

Verify Before You Click

Hover over links to preview URLs before clicking. Check the sender email carefully — phishing often uses look-alike addresses.

05

Avoid Public Wi-Fi

Use a VPN on public networks. Never access banking or sensitive accounts on unsecured Wi-Fi hotspots.

06

Back Up Your Data

Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies, on 2 different media, with 1 off-site. This protects you from ransomware and hardware failure.

07

Use a Password Manager

Tools like Bitwarden or KeePass generate and store unique passwords for every site so you only need to remember one master password.

08

Check for HTTPS

Always look for the padlock icon and "https://" before entering any personal or payment information on a website.
